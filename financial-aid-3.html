California Dream Act (CDA) Application

The California Dream Act application allows eligible students enrolled in California Colleges, Universities and Career Education Programs to apply for state and institutional financial aid.

The California Dream Act (CDA) application is oversee by the California Student Aid Commission (CSAC)Opens in new window and should be completed by students who may be eligible for California state aid programs only under the AB540 status.

CSUF School Code: 001137

Preparing to Apply

To get started with the CDA application process you should know you have two options to complete the application:

  1. Online Application - Highly Recommended as the application process is faster and easier.
  2. Paper Application - If you do not have a computer with Internet access at home, you can find Internet access at your local library, high school, or college financial aid office.

Gathering Info | Dependent Student (Yours & Parents) | Independent Students (Only Yours)

  1. W-2 forms and other records of income
  2. Income Tax Returns (For more information if you or your parents returns have not been completed, follow the instructions on the Dream Act application)
  3. Records of child support paid (if applicable)
  4. Current bank statements for assets (cash, saving) as of the date you completed your application (if applicable)
  5. Current stock, bond, and other investment records (if applicable)
  6. Current business and farm records (if applicable)

A recommendation if you are applying online:

  1. Before going online, print out and complete the Dream Act application worksheet. This will help you complete the application.
  2. Then, go to the CDA application. You will first establish your user name and password before being guided through a step-by-step application process.

“California Dream Act application is not shared with Federal Databases. Student and parents information is protected by the same privacy and information security laws and safeguards as all other state financial aid applicants.”

- Information from the California Student Aid Commission (CSAC)
